Aliens Live in Arsenic!

BY HELEN KENNEDY / DAILY NEWS STAFF WRITER
Updated: Friday, December 3, 2010, 1:18 PM

NASA announced Thursday it has discovered alien life in California's Mono Lake, bacteria that use arsenic instead of phosphorus in its DNA. All life discovered so far from teeny amoebas to enormous elephants, are composed of the same six elements: carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, oxygen, sulfur, and phosphorus. But Felisa Wolfe-Simon, a scientist at Arizona State University's School of Earth and Space Exploration, found a strain of Halomonadaceae bacteria called GFAJ-1 on the bottom of California's volcanic Mono Lake that can live without any phosphorus. Instead, when deprived of phosphorus, instead of dying, it uses arsenic to build cells.

NASA planetary scientist Pamela Conrad compared it to the classic Star Trek episode about discovering a cave-dwelling lifeform based on silicon instead of carbon. "This is the equivalent of finding that Horta," Conrad said. "This is a huge deal!"
